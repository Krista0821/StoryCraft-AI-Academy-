Define Your Exact Reader

Lock in your target age group and understand exactly who you're writing for

⏱ 10 Min
Why This Matters
Children's books are not "for kids." They're for a very specific window of development. A book aimed at a 3-year-old looks, sounds, and reads completely differently than one for an 8-year-old. Choosing the wrong range means wrong vocabulary, wrong length, and wrong illustrations — making your book hard to sell.
Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. 1Read the three age ranges below carefully. Do not skip this.
  2. 2Circle or mark the age range that best fits the story idea forming in your head, OR the audience you most want to reach.
  3. 3Write down ONE deciding reason for your choice in the space provided.
Age Range Reference Guide
🧒 Ages 2–4 · Board Book / Picture Book
Word count: 100–500 words
Structure: Repetition, rhythm, rhyme
Themes: Emotions, bedtime, animals, colors, counting
Who buys it: Parents of toddlers, daycare, preschool
🎒 Ages 5–7 · Early Picture Book
Word count: 500–1,000 words
Structure: Simple arc, clear problem + solution
Themes: School, friendship, fears, kindness, humor
Who buys it: Kindergarten–Grade 1 parents, teachers
📚 Ages 8–10 · Chapter / Illustrated Story
Word count: 1,000–10,000 words
Structure: Chapters, more complex character growth
Themes: Adventure, identity, problem-solving, values
Who buys it: Parents of early readers, schools, libraries

Activity 3 of 5
03

Fast Amazon Demand Check

Validate your idea in under 10 minutes using Amazon's own search data

⏱ 10 Min
Why This Matters
You don't need expensive market research tools. Amazon tells you exactly what's selling. If books like yours exist and are ranked — that's proof of demand, not competition to fear. If nothing like yours exists, that's a warning sign, not an opportunity.
Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. 1Go to Amazon.com and click the search bar. Set the department to Books.
  2. 2Type in a 3–5 word phrase describing your book idea. Example: "courage book for kids" or "bedtime story ages 4."
  3. 3Look at the first 10 results. Count how many books closely match your topic.
  4. 4Click on 2–3 books. Check their Best Seller Rank (BSR) — found under "Product Details." Lower number = more sales.
  5. 5Look at the book covers, titles, and descriptions. Note what they're doing well — or what's missing that you could do better.
  6. 6Record your findings below.
BSR Quick Reference
Best Seller Rank (BSR)What It MeansSignal
#1 – #10,000 Selling daily — strong demand in this niche ✅ Go
#10,001 – #100,000 Selling weekly — moderate but viable demand ⚠️ Proceed with twist
#100,001+ Selling rarely — low demand or oversaturated ❌ Reconsider
No results found No market — pivot your topic immediately ❌ Pivot
Your Amazon Research Notes
🚫 Time limit is strict: 10 minutes maximum. Set a timer. Do not fall into a research rabbit hole. You are looking for a signal, not writing a market report. One search, 2–3 book checks, done.
Activity 4 of 5
04

Cliché Filter — Pass or Pivot

Stress-test your idea against the most oversaturated children's book topics

⏱ 5 Min
Why This Matters
Thousands of children's books about dinosaurs learning to share, unicorns being brave, and dragons who are different exist on Amazon right now. Most never sell. Your idea doesn't need to be 100% original — but it does need a distinguishing twist, angle, or perspective that sets it apart. If your idea is on the cliché list with no twist, change it now — before you spend 7 days creating it.
Oversaturated Topic Checklist

Check if your idea overlaps with any of these. If it does, you need a twist — or a pivot.

  • Generic "be yourself" or "you are special" message
  • Dinosaur learning an emotion (sharing, kindness, etc.)
  • Unicorn / magical creature overcoming a fear
  • Dragon who is "different from the others"
  • Generic ABC or 123 counting book (no niche or theme)
  • "First day of school" anxiety story (standard format)
  • "Monsters aren't real" bedtime reassurance story
  • Generic friendship story with animal characters
Twist It or Drop It
If your idea matches anything above, write your UNIQUE TWIST below. What makes yours completely different? (Specific setting? Unexpected character? Counter-intuitive lesson? Humor angle?)
💡
The Twist Rule: You don't need a brand new idea. You need a better angle on an existing idea. Example: Instead of "dinosaur learns to share" → "dinosaur who hates vegetables accidentally grows a garden and has to share it with the whole neighborhood." Same emotion, completely different hook.

Your Pitch
A shy turtle who wants to join the school talent show discovers that asking for help isn't giving up — it's the bravest thing you can do.
When Maya's science project goes hilariously wrong, she learns that every great discovery starts with a mistake — and a second try.
Write Your Concept Pitch
  1. 1Use the formula above. Fill in the blanks from what you've decided in Activities 1–4.
  2. 2Write a rough version first, then refine it once.
  3. 3Read it out loud. If it takes more than 2 sentences, cut it down.
  4. 4Ask yourself: "Would I buy this book for a child I know?" If yes — you're done.
